The simple rule: If it looks warm, earthy, or golden — skip it. If it looks very stark, icy, or intensely saturated, it may be pushing too far into Winter. Your sweet spot is cool, clear, and balanced. Not too muted, not too vivid. Think: "Cool always. Balanced in everything else."

You sit between True Summer and True Winter. Cool Summer is a flow season, firmly rooted in Summer's cool, composed quality, but with Winter's influence bringing more clarity and depth. Your colours don't need to be as muted as True Summer, and they won't be as stark or icy as True Winter. You share colours with both neighbours and that's exactly the point. If a colour works for True Summer or True Winter, there's a good chance it works for you, especially where they overlap: cool, relatively clear, and medium in depth.

This is a guide, not a rulebook. Every colour in your palette can be worn anywhere: clothing, makeup, nail polish, jewellery, eyewear, ties, hats, scarves and beyond. Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring, then trust yourself. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

Your palette is expansive. The Cool Summer palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches here are carefully chosen examples to help you understand the qualities to look for: cool, clear, and composed. When shopping, use these as a visual compass, not a checklist. If a colour shares those same qualities, it likely belongs to you.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher before browsing your palette. On iPhone, look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung, look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display. On other Android devices it may be called Night Light or Blue Light Filter under Settings > Display.

Online shopping tip: When shopping online and unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant with the brand, product, and colour name included. For example: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] cool or warm toned? Does it suit Cool Summer?" Then do a visual check by comparing the item against your palette. Use AI as a starting point. It has limitations and pulls from what is available online, so always check samples in store when possible, contact the brand directly, or ask staff for help.

Scarves, Hats & Wraps
Near-face accessories matter most

A powder blue scarf, a dusty rose hat, or a lavender wrap near your face will all be flattering. Tip: a cool-toned scarf near the face can transform an otherwise neutral outfit into something that truly works for your season.

For the Men
Ties, pocket squares & accessories
The same palette applies. Your Cool Summer colours translate directly into ties, pocket squares, cufflinks, and accessories. A soft navy or steel blue tie is a classic power choice. Charcoal and soft grey suits are your strongest base. Silver cufflinks over gold. A cool white shirt over cream. A French blue or periwinkle tie over brown or olive.
Makeup & Hair
Your Cool Summer guide to beauty. Cool, composed, and clear, with more depth and definition than True Summer. Shades listed are longstanding classics worth looking for.
Your golden rule: If it looks warm, peachy, golden, or brown-toned, put it back. Cool Summer makeup plays in the middle, not the pale dusty softness of True Summer, and not the vivid intensity of True Winter. Think cool blues, clear berries, medium pinks and purples with real colour payoff but without harshness. This is a playful, confident mid-range that leans cool in every direction.
Lips
Your most flattering lip colours are cool-based and clearly pigmented: cool rose, clear berry, and cool mauve all sit in your mid-range sweet spot. You have room to go deeper and more defined than True Summer, but you're not chasing the vivid blue-reds of True Winter. Think cool and clear, with real colour presence.

Your "nude" lip: cool, not beige
Traditional warm nudes wash you out. Your version of a natural lip is a sheer cool pink, soft rose, or cool mauve balm. Look for shades described as cool rose, cool mauve, or sheer berry. Try: ILIA Tinted Lip Conditioner, RMS Beauty Wild Roses worn lightly, or a cool rose lip liner worn alone.
Blush

Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Your most important foundation rule: cool or neutral undertone only. Pink-based or neutral foundations work best. Yellow or golden-based foundations will fight your natural colouring.
What to look for
Ask for pink, cool, or neutral undertone in your shade. Avoid shades described as golden, warm, yellow, or honey. For tinted moisturisers, ILIA Skin Tint and RMS Beauty "Un" Cover-Up are great natural options. Tip: when testing foundation on your wrist, a match that disappears means neutral. One that looks orange or yellow means it's too warm for you.
Eyes
Your eyes have real room to play in the blues, purples, and cool greys. Think periwinkle lid washes, clear lavender, steel grey, and soft navy, these sit right in your mid-range and bring out the cool clarity in your colouring. You can go defined and expressive here without tipping into True Winter sharpness. Skip warm browns entirely, they work against your coolness.
Eyeshadow: what works
Base shades: soft white, pale lavender, silver grey, cool rose-beige. Depth: soft navy, dusty violet, cool charcoal, slate grey, cool plum. Accents: periwinkle, clear lavender, steel blue, soft silver, dusty mauve. Satin and soft metallic finishes suit your mid-range placement beautifully, they add dimension without harshness. Avoid: gold, bronze, warm brown, terracotta, peach, or any warm metallic.
Eyeliner
Soft charcoal or cool grey is your most versatile liner. Navy is beautiful for a soft smoky effect. If black feels too sharp, opt for a cool graphite or soft dark grey. Avoid brown liner, which introduces warmth. Tip: a smudged navy or grey liner with a clear berry lip is a quintessential Cool Summer look: cool and expressive without being stark.
Mascara
Black or dark navy mascara is your best choice. Soft charcoal works too. Brown mascara introduces warmth and dilutes your natural cool contrast. Try: ILIA Fullest Volumizing Mascara in black, NYX Worth the Hype in black, or Ere Perez Almond Oil Mascara in black.
Brows & Beard
Brow guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour, or go one shade darker. Look for cool taupe, soft cool brown, or cool dark brown depending on your natural colouring. Avoid anything warm, golden, or auburn-toned, these fight your natural cool colouring right at the frame of your face where it matters most. Tip: ask for "cool" or "cool-toned" when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Beard & grooming colour
If you colour your beard or brows, look for cooler tones. Cool brown, cool medium brown, or soft black work well. Avoid anything with a warm, reddish, golden, or auburn tone. When in doubt, go one shade cooler rather than warmer, and always do a strand test first. Tip: the same cool-toned guidance applies here as it does for hair colour.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair colour is often the one you were born with. Cool Summer naturally spans a wide range, from light cool blonde through to cool medium brown and even deeper cool browns. Whatever your natural depth, the quality is the same: a softness to the overall look, as though the face is gently lit rather than starkly contrasted. That cool, diffused quality is what makes this placement so beautiful, at any depth.
Your best hair colours
Your naturals: cool blonde, cool beige blonde, cool medium brown, cool dark brown, the full range works, as long as the tone stays cool and ashy rather than warm or golden. Lighter options: cool blonde or platinum, always cool, never golden. Deeper options: cool dark brown works beautifully, look for shades with an ashy or blue-brown base rather than red, warm, or chestnut. Avoid: golden highlights, copper, red tones, warm auburn, caramel, or honey, these introduce warmth that fights your natural cool quality.
Embracing silver & grey
Silver and grey hair can be absolutely stunning on Cool Summer. It mirrors the cool, soft elegance of your palette beautifully. If you are going silver naturally, consider leaning into it rather than covering it. Tip: a purple or blue toning shampoo keeps it cool and luminous rather than yellow.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "cool toned," "no warmth," or show them your palette directly, and having your actual cool colour reference makes the conversation easier and avoids misunderstandings. Tip: when in doubt, go cooler and softer rather than warmer and brighter.
Shopping tip: When you find a product you love but are not sure if it works, try asking an AI assistant: "Is [Brand Name] [Product Name] in [Shade Name] cool or warm toned? Is it suitable for Cool Summer?" Then do a visual check by comparing the product swatch against your palette. Trust your eye.
